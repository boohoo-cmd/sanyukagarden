The Kingdom of Buganda

Dining on royal ground

Sanyuka rests in the gardens of Mengo Palace — the heart of one of Africa's oldest kingdoms.

For over five centuries, Buganda has shaped the culture, the food, and the soul of this land. To eat here is to be a guest of that living history.

A Kingdom of Legacy

Centuries of royal lineage

Buganda's story spans more than 500 years, carried through a succession of Kabakas who guided the kingdom through growth, challenge, and cultural flowering — building one of East Africa's most influential powers.

The Kabaka remains a revered figure today, a symbol of continuity and pride. And Mengo Palace, where Sanyuka stands, is the ground where that story lives.

Sacred Ground

Mengo Palace

On Lubiri Hill stands the palace of the Kabaka — the symbolic and administrative heart of Buganda. Its grounds are sacred, its gardens filled with trees that have stood for over a century.

These gardens have witnessed pivotal moments in the kingdom's history. Today they offer a rare thing in the city: stillness, shade, and a deep sense of place.

The Buganda Kitchen

A cuisine of the soil

Buganda food is built on fresh, seasonal, local ingredients and time-honoured methods passed down through generations.

Matooke — steamed plantain, the heart of nearly every Buganda meal.

Luwombo — meat steamed in banana leaves, a dish of celebration.

Katogo — the morning dish of plantain and beans, shared among family.

We keep these alive on our menu, prepared with the respect they deserve.

Our Promise

Sanyuka is a gift to Kampala — a place to sit beneath the same trees that have watched over the kingdom for generations, and to taste a heritage worth keeping alive.
